Kasaragod

Kasaragod is a municipal, town and district headquarters of Kasaragod district lies in the Kerala state. It is the well known destination place for the tourists in Kerala. It is situated on the bank of the sea with all the facility. Here you easily spend two or three days because there are lots of beautiful places like Ananthapura Lake Temple, Kasaragod beach, Kappil beach, Mogral river etc. These all the places simply takes your half day. The Ananthapura temple is totally enclosed by the lake. About 50 meters from the lake there is a pond in which crocodiles are nursed. These crocodiles are well known and trained by the devotees of the temple. Crocodiles never attack on anyone, they also eat only offers by the devotees of the temple. This show the kindness and strongest tradition of the temple and they believe in God. Another exciting place here is the beach and river where you can find the peace in the mid. These are the place where you can find the awesome scenario of the nature with enjoying the beautiful place. Bekal fort is also a very unbelievable place there. It is the historical spot with some of the area is submerged in the water.

Bekal Fort

Bekal fort is the largest fort of Kerala situated in the Bekal village in Kasaragod district lies in the northern part of Kerala. The most beautiful thing is it situated on the sea beach that attract tourist so much. Due to the fantastic look of the whole scenario tourists love to come there. There are some more things to visit like waterfall, hill station, backwater and etc. This fort is shaped just like as the keyhole and the overall look is so beautiful with the Arabian Sea. This fort is so old and half of the part of them is in sea water

Thaikadappuram Beach

There are not many people in the world who can boast of catching a glimpse of the rare Olive Ridley Turtles. But a visit to the Thaikadappuram Beach, near Nileshwaram in Kasaragod district of Kerala, might give you the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity of catching a glimpse of them. The beach is the breeding ground of these remarkable creatures who visit every monsoon season to lay eggs. Activists of Neythal, a non-governmental organisation, run an Olive Ridley breeding centre here. The ideal time to visit the area, situated 40 km south of Kasaragod, is September to January.

Ranipuram Hills

Ranipuram is a renowned tourist destination in the northern tip of Kerala. Located in Kasaragod, it is situated 750 m above sea level. It makes for a perfect picnic spot where one can even come across the occasional herd of elephants. Once known as Madathumala, it borders Karnataka and boasts of some of the best trekking trails in the area. Regular buses are available on this route and jeep rides are another favourite among all our visitors. The versatile vegetation that includes evergreen shola woods, monsoon forests and grasslands make it a good place to relax and take some time off from the rigors of daily life.
